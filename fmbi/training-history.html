Brief description - Discussion about mental focus

Place - Vasant Kunj Office

We had a discussion about what basically focus is? What all does 'focus' involves. So we took the example of 'focusing on the cup while looking somewhere else' or without looking at the cup at all. This example explained that to focus on something, a person needs to atleast 'look' at that thing, along with thinking or feeling it.

This gave us one important task to do â€“ 'Eye control' Before we can control our minds from wandering, it is important to have a control over our eyes. And I should watch neutral things and may be just no where like somewhere long distance but not watching anything specific. Don't have to meet anybody's eyes during training and matches. I need to have a complete control over my eyes.

Another important thing discussed was to be aware that even 'concentration' requires a lot of effort. We cannot assume that it will come naturally or without effort. The fact is, I will have to make an extra effort to focus on what I want to focus on.

Then we discussed about the 3 types of focus i experience during my training:

Type I â€“ where I am completely distracted and lose control on my technique. This is the 'no focus' or 'zero focus' stage. Stage I focus â€“ Where I am not distracted and I consciously make effort to focus on my technique. I keep a check on all technical steps required during a shot or a series. Here I do reasonably good. This is the stage where I can work consciously on my technique and also improve on the technique.

Stage II focus â€“ In this kind of focus, I am not distracted and I don't even make any extra efforts to focus consciously on my technique. Everything just happens on its own and I am just wanting to feel the way I love to shoot. I feel empty minded here and I am just able to do the things right automatically. Here I am not working to improve on my technique, like in Stage I focus â€“ but I am just re-doing what I've learnt so far, with 'feeling' being the main element here. Almost at all times, I shoot really good during this kind of focus. Whenever I am able to achieve it.
