Set of tools for focusing your mind

Confluence of scientific instrumentation & Yogic theory of concentration of mind

Yoga is about stilling of fluctuations of mind and developing sharply focused state of mind.

When we want to achieve anything in life - be it sports, career, academics, personal life, or self-discovery through meditation, we need to concentrate or full attention on it.

We cannot achieve anything in life without concentration of mind.

When you set out on your journey, it is easy to lose focus of your goal from time to time and go off the track.

This is because there are several challenges to mind's focus.

Similar challenges were identified by Yogi Patanjali in his book Yoga Pradeepika â€“challenges to concentration of mind in meditation.

Theory

Our state of mind changes from time to time. Sometimes we feel very strong, sometimes weak. On some days our self control is very strong, we can resist all temptations, sometimes we can't. ON some days we are in a flow, we finish large amount of work, some days we can't even get out of bed. Some days we are convinced about our goals, we fully believe in ourselves, some days there are clouds of doubt. So our state of mind is never constant. These are the fluctuations of mind. These are called vritti

Yoga is the stilling of the changing states of mind.(Sutra 1.01)

When these fluctuations cease to exist, you achieve a mental state of absorption. This focused state of mind is very powerful. And you discover your nature.

However there are several disturbances which prevent you from developing this state of mind (Sutra 1.30) -

Idleness (Styana)

Doubt /indecision (Samsaya)

Carelessness/negligence (pramada)

Sloth/laziness (Sloth)

Confusion (bhranthi)

Lack of detachment (avirati)

Not attaining a base (bhumikatva)

Instability of mind(anavasthitatvani)

Disease/injury (vyadhi)

In order to achieve deep concentration, you need to overcome these disturbances through regular practice and make a conscious effort to fix the mind on one object.
